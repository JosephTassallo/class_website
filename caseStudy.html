Case Study on Department of Labor

objective

The objective of this project was to redesign the Department of Labor’s website to make it one; more visually appealing, and two; create a smoother user flow.

preliminary Research

The main takeaways we received from these user tests were that the website was unorganized and overwhelming as well as visually unappealing to users. Users found it very easy to get lost. They found the experience to be overwelming and stressful.

design process

Our first step was to identify our audience through the creation of our user persona. From this we built out the organizational structure for the site. The biggest priority was the structure of the drop-down menu. With a total of 39 links under the “topics” tab it was extremely overwhelming. We ran card sorting exercises to better understand how users would like to see these topics structured. From this we were able to drop from 39 links down to 5 with sub-topics on a secondary page. The addition of quick links on the home page also relieved some of this stress put on the user. This responded much better within the user tests.

design iterations

With our process we focused on accessibility with it being a government website. We wanted to assure that our users were able to read, interact, and understand the information that was displayed. The feedback we received from users was positive.
